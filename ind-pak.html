India-Pak Relations

Jan 6th 2020 

Relations between India and Pakistan have been complex and largely hostile due to a number of historical and political events. Relations between the two states have been defined by the violent partition of British India in 1947, the Kashmir conflict, and the numerous military conflicts fought between the two nations. Consequently, their relationship has been plagued by hostility and suspicion.

Key Points

1.In the year 1947 , two countries India and Pakistan got independence. Both the 
countries were democratic countries.But just after independence we fought a war with Pakistan in the year 1948 , 1965 , 1971 ,1999 and the reason was Kashmir conflict.  

2 .Since early 1980 the relations between India and Pakistan got even more worse due to Siachen conflict, Kashmir insurgency in 1989 ,India-Pakistan nuclear war test 1998 and Kargil war 1999.
 
3 .But to promote peace along India- Pakistan border , India took various steps 
for example:
     .Shimla Agreement (1972)
     .Agra Treaty
    .Lahore Declaration
    
4 .Even after this, the relations between India and Pakistan did not flourish.

5 .The bilateral trade between India and Pakistan is only of  2.29 billion dollar.
